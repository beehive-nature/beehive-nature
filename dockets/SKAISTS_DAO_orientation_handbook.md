## 1. WHAT THIS DAO DOES ON DAY ONE

**It commissions laboratory measurements that nobody else will pay for, and publishes every result.**

That is the founding responsibility. Not token economics, not treasury allocation — **buying measurements the market has left unbought, and releasing them.**

**Why this and not something else:** the testing market measures what is *ordered*, and ordering is driven by regulatory minimums, cost per line item, and liability avoidance — **never by what is worth knowing.** No single grower funds a complete panel because every downstream buyer captures the benefit. **That is commons underinvestment, and a DAO is the correct shape for a cost whose benefit is diffuse.**

**The diagnosis is "under-commissioned," never "corrupt."** Laboratories largely work. What fails is who buys what, at what floor. Say it that way — it is what the evidence supports, and it leaves every result we publish usable. *(If we called labs corrupt, our own published COAs would be worthless by our own argument.)*

## 2. THE DOCTRINE — every step converts a claim into an exhibit

**This is the one thing to carry out of this handbook.**

Law 1 says a universal requires an exhibited witness. **Fundraising, partnership and publication are the same discipline in different clothes.** Every step below takes something you *assert* and turns it into something a stranger can *check*:

| You want to claim | The exhibit that replaces the claim |
|---|---|
| "There is a gap here" | a **recorded negative search** — where you looked, and what was not there |
| "This can be measured" | **one lot, measured** |
| "People need this" | **letters from the people who would use it** |
| "We will publish everything" | **a pre-registered publication commitment, made before the sample ships** |
| "We know how" | **a method-cited specification** with LOQs and acceptance criteria |

**A proposal made of claims competes on rhetoric. A proposal made of exhibits competes on evidence, and there is far less competition there.**

## 3. THE SIX STEPS — taking a measurement gap to a funded round

### Step 1 · Verify the gap, and record the negative result

**Go looking for the data properly, then write down that you did not find it** — sources searched, databases queried, what each contained. **A documented absence is evidence; an assumed absence is a guess.**

*Most proposals assert a gap. Being able to demonstrate one is rare and it is the strongest opening you have.*

### Step 2 · Write the specification before you ask anyone for money

**Analytes, methods with standard numbers, LOQ gates, acceptance criteria, and what constitutes a non-conforming result.** *(The hulled-hemp SPEC is the template.)*

**Most applicants propose to develop methods. Arriving with them already written reads as competence and costs only your time.**

**Non-negotiables in any spec:** no blank cells (a blank is a rejection) · every non-result carries its reason · every `<LOQ` states its LOQ inline · replicates reported individually with RSD, never pooled.

### Step 3 · Approach the people who maintain the standard you are extending

**This is the highest-leverage step and the most commonly skipped.**

**Whatever schema you are extending has authors, and they are your warmest possible contact.** Not *"fund my project"* — **"your standard has no complete panel behind it; I have written the spec; would you look at it?"**

**Find them in the acknowledgments.** Standards documents name their contributors — often dozens of people who already care about exactly this problem. *(The USDA Hemp Descriptor Handbook acknowledges roughly fifty named researchers across a dozen land-grant institutions. That is a pipeline, not a coincidence.)*

**Tradeoff, stated honestly:** an institutional co-investigator usually means **their institution becomes applicant of record and holds the award.** Better odds, less control. **For a first round that is often the right trade — decide it deliberately rather than discovering it at submission.**

### Step 4 · Get quotes before you get ambitious

**No budget, no application.** Request itemised quotations, and require each lab to name the analytes it **cannot** perform. **Scope follows funding, never the reverse** — decide the lot count after the number arrives, not before.

### Step 5 · Measure one thing yourself

**One lot, self-funded, at full spec, published openly.**

**This converts "we propose to measure" into "we measured, here is what we found, here is why it must scale"** — frequently the decisive difference in competitive review.

**And it does double duty: it is simultaneously preliminary data and a working demonstration that the institution does what it says.** Anyone can promise unconditional publication. One published lot proves it.

### Step 6 · Assemble the exhibits

**Letters of support** from growers, processors and buyers who would use the data — two or three materially help mission-weighted programmes.

**Data management plan** — repository, open licence, format, submission path. **Note: our publication commitment already *is* the DMP.** The control written for governance reasons satisfies the funder requirement without additional work. *(It also happens to be close to the operative test for exempt scientific research — results made available to the public on a nondiscriminatory basis.)*

## 4. THE FOUR CONTROLS — binding on every commissioning, no exceptions

Full text at `RELAY_14 §1b`. In brief:

| | Control |
|---|---|
| **C1** | **Publication is pre-committed before the sample ships and binds regardless of result.** Commissioning and publishing are one indivisible act. |
| **C2** | **Commission lots the DAO does not control** — imported, competitor, third-party — on the same spec. *A body that only tests what it sells is producing marketing.* |
| **C3** | **Spec and commissioning are separate powers.** You vote what to commission, never how to specify it. Amending the spec is a distinct, higher-bar act. |
| **C4** | **No laboratory in which the DAO, the founder, or a representative holds an interest.** Independence is the product. |

**C1 is the one that will be tested.** The pressure arrives sounding reasonable — *"let's see the results first,"* *"we'll publish once it's cleaned up,"* *"this round we'll just do our own lots, it's cheaper."* **Each of those rebuilds, inside this institution, the exact liability-avoidance-by-omission it exists to defeat.**

## 5. HOW A COMMISSIONING ACTUALLY MOVES

```
member proposes  →  circles deliberate + vote  →  bQueenBee records the vote,
                                                   verifies C1–C4 mechanically
                                                        ↓
                                        conformance-checked recommendation
                                                        ↓
                                    BOARD DECIDES  (humans hold the duty)
                                                        ↓
                                    bQueenBee records decision + any override
                                                   and its stated reasons
```

**`bQueenBee` custodies the process. Humans hold the duty.** She cannot be a fiduciary — software cannot be sued, removed, or held accountable — but she is what lets a director *prove* they were diligent.

**And the control that keeps it honest: a board that has never overridden `bQueenBee` is not governing.** The override path must exist, be used when warranted, and be recorded with reasons.

*This is the same law you will meet everywhere here: a lint never shown to fail is not a lint · a debate surface where nothing resolves low is advocacy · a board that never overrides is a rubber stamp. **Every mechanism proves itself only by returning the inconvenient answer sometimes.***

## 6. WORKED EXAMPLE — the first round

**Gap:** no complete lot-level composition COA for hulled hemp seed exists publicly, in any jurisdiction. *Searched; negative result recorded.*

**Spec:** proximate · fatty acids (ISO 12966-1) · unsaponifiables · amino acids (three hydrolyses) · minerals + heavy metals · vitamins · **antinutrients** · cannabinoids at **LOQ ≤ 0.1 µg/g** · contaminants · soil panel.

**The science question underneath it:** *does hulled hemp's mineral content survive its phytate load, and does that vary by origin?* **The dataset is the deliverable; the bioavailability question is the research.**

**Design:** 3 jurisdictions × 3 lots × 3 replicates, jurisdictions chosen from actual US import share.

**Status:** RFQ out · fiscal sponsor sought · academic collaborator sought · grant path Farm Aid and state programmes first, AFRI after preliminary data.

## 7. WHAT YOU DO NOT NEED PERMISSION FOR

**Verifying a gap. Reading a standard. Writing a draft spec. Emailing the people who wrote the schema. Getting quotes.**

**None of that spends money or binds the DAO, and all of it is the work.** Bring the exhibits to the circles; that is what a proposal is here.

**What needs a vote:** commissioning anything, amending a spec, and any expenditure.
