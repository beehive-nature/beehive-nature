# FOUNDER-RIGHT-1 — The Representation Right

**Status: RATIFIED — v1.0, 2026-07-19. Epoch-0 founder ratification.**
**Companion to `CONSTITUTION.md`, which it does not amend.** The seven primitives and Article II's invariants bind unchanged. This document governs one authority held by one named person, and the conditions under which it ends.

---

## 1. What this document settles

**Who decides which persons may represent for-profit capital entities operating within the BNR ecosystem — and, more importantly, when that authority stops.**

**It is published before it vests.** The right does not begin until the Injective plugin demo goes live; that timestamp does not yet exist. **Disclosing a constraint before holding the thing it constrains is the only version of this document worth writing** — an authority disclosed after it is exercised is a confession, not a constitution.

---

## 2. The right

**Holder:** Travis Mark Remington, founder and sole human inventor.

**Scope:** approval of who may represent **Capital Business For Profit entities** within the BNR ecosystem.

**Vests:** at the recorded live-launch timestamp of the Injective plugin demo. **Not before.**

---

## 3. What this right is NOT — the bounds are the point

**Enumerated, so the right cannot be read wider than it is:**

| It does **not** confer | Governed instead by |
|---|---|
| Kernel amendment authority | Article VI, and its epochs — a separate authority with a separate sunset (§7) |
| **Any governance weight whatsoever** | **GOV-1: weight is denominated in Respect, only** |
| Respect, or any path to accruing it | `reputation-engine`, from ledgered Evidence |
| Authority over a 501(c)(3) board's fiduciary decisions | non-delegable, held by directors |
| Authority over the DAO's commissioning votes | the circles, and the four controls |
| Power to compel any person to accept representation | **consent is the attestor — §6** |
| Any economic claim, fee, or preference | nothing; the right is unpaid |

**This right is a veto over representation and nothing else.**

---

## 4. The dual gate — how it ends

**It expires at whichever gate trips first:**

**(i) DATE — thirteen years** from the recorded vesting timestamp.

**(ii) TRIGGER — Epoch-2 entry**, verified against `reputation-engine` state: *≥ 34% of live Respect participating, averaged over 13 rounds* (Art. VI §3.5). **Verified, never declared.**

**Why a trigger at all.** Article VI §3.0 governs how authority moves here: *"in epochs on measurable triggers, **never dates and never promises**."* **A bare date is the pattern this constitution rejects**, and it rejects it for a stated reason — legitimacy cannot be added retroactively.

**Why Epoch 2 specifically.** Epoch 2 is when the founder's *kernel* veto sunsets under §3.5. Binding this right to the same threshold produces **one sunset, not two**: founder authority ends across kernel and capital at a single measurable moment, on a threshold the constitution already defines, already measures, and already meta-locks. **A fresh number would add a parameter nobody audits.**

**On expiry, by either gate:** the right is **destroyed publicly and the destruction is ledgered as an Event** — the pattern Article VI §3.7 uses for the guardian keys. **Sovereignty as a replayable fact on the bus, forever.**

**Movement is one-way. There is no re-vesting path**, per §3.5.

## 5. Disclosure while it stands

**This right is an authority-axis concentration and every surface must compute it as one.**

- Any surface asserting a decentralisation level **reads this right as live** until expiry.
- **On expiry the level rises automatically and visibly.** The sunset is watchable, not announced.
- **Negative control:** a surface asserting an authority-axis level that ignores an unexpired FOUNDER-RIGHT-1 → **fail**.

**A disclosed, dated, expiring concentration is more credible than an unexamined claim of decentralisation, because it is checkable.** The projects that are caught are the ones that hid it.

---

## 6. Representatives — consent is the attestor

**No person is a representative of any BNR capital entity until they have signed.** The holder's approval is necessary and never sufficient.

**BNR does not assert that a person represents it. It exhibits their signature, or it exhibits nothing.** There is no `Verified` variant here, as there is none anywhere else in this system.

**Every `Representative` record carries a uniform disclosure block** — material legal, regulatory and financial history — **and the schema applies to every record equally, including the holder's own.** A field every record fills is not a spotlight on anyone.

**Either side may revoke at any time.** A representative may resign; the holder may withdraw approval. Neither requires cause.

## 7. Relationship to the Article VI founder authority

**Two authorities, held by one person, ending separately:**

| | FOUNDER-RIGHT-1 | Article VI §3.6/§3.7 |
|---|---|---|
| Governs | representation of capital entities | kernel amendment: Epoch-1 veto, meta-tier co-sign |
| Ends at | 13 years **or** Epoch-2 entry | the veto sunsets at Epoch 2; the co-sign releases on §3.7's three conditions |

**Neither extends the other. Neither revives the other. Expiry of one has no effect on the other.**

**If Epoch 2 arrives before year thirteen, both the kernel veto and this right end at the same measured moment** — which is the intended design.

## 8. Amendment

**Widening the scope of this right, extending either gate, or creating any re-vesting path is a META-TIER amendment** — Article VI §3.3, `K = 8`, 21% affirmative quorum, founder/guardian co-sign through Epoch 1.

**Narrowing the scope, shortening either gate, or early voluntary release requires only the holder's signature**, ledgered as an Event.

**The asymmetry is deliberate: it is always easier to give this away than to keep it.**
